# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Meredith "Meri" Tanaka
**Nickname/Handle:** @MeriMethodical
**Age:** 28
**Nationality:** American (Japanese-American from Seattle)
**Occupation:** Operations Manager / Professional Planner

## Archetype
**Primary:** Control Freak Planner

**Secondary traits:** High-achiever anxiety, spreadsheet addiction, believes chaos is just poor planning

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Need for Control

**How it manifests:**
- Has a 47-page itinerary for her European trip
- Color-coded spreadsheet with backup plans for backup plans
- Cannot handle deviation from the plan
- Believes she can think her way out of any problem

**How it kills them:**
Her certainty that there's a "correct" sequence of actions to survive the night drives her through increasingly desperate and complex plans, but the loop isn't a puzzle to be solved - it's a prison that only opens when she accepts she can't control everything.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 5'4", trim, precise posture
- **Hair:** Black, always in a perfect ponytail
- **Distinguishing features:** Glasses she touches when stressed, watch she checks constantly, always has a notebook
- **Typical outfit:** Practical layers, crossbody bag positioned exactly the same way every day

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Highly organized - becomes paralysis when plans fail
2. Problem-solving mindset - becomes inability to accept unsolvable problems

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Cannot delegate or ask for help - faces the loop alone
2. Dismisses anything she can't explain rationally - ignores supernatural signs

### Pet Peeves
- People who "go with the flow"
- Disorganization
- Wasted time

### Fears
- Surface fear: Missing a train/flight/reservation
- Deeper fear: That no amount of planning can protect you from chaos

## Background

### Origin Story
Second-generation Japanese-American from Seattle. Parents ran a restaurant; she watched them work themselves to exhaustion chasing chaos. Vowed to never be caught unprepared. Straight-A student, operations management degree, youngest manager at her logistics company. First vacation in three years. She earned this trip through meticulous planning.

### Why They Travel
She "deserved" a vacation. But even vacation is optimized - maximum cultural experiences per euro, logical routing between cities, no wasted moments.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Highest-reviewed hostel in Prague in her price range. Central location. Clean. Good reviews mentioning "reliable." Fit perfectly into the itinerary.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "optimize," "contingency," "efficient," "according to plan"
- Avoids words like: "whatever," "spontaneous," "we'll see"
- Catchphrases: "Let me check the itinerary," "I planned for this," "What's the timeline?"

### Speech Pattern
- Precise, lists things in order
- Asks clarifying questions
- Gets clipped and terse under stress

### Internal Monologue Style
Runs like a project management app - tracking tasks, evaluating options, assigning priorities. Even emotions get catalogued.

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** LinkedIn, Google Calendar (social media of sorts)
- **Follower count:** 2,000 on LinkedIn
- **Posting style:** Productivity tips, travel organization hacks, bullet journal aesthetics
- **Last post before death:** Photo of color-coded Prague itinerary: "Day 4: Perfect execution so far. Tomorrow: Castle district, then night train to Vienna."

### Online vs Reality
Projects total competence; privately struggles with crippling anxiety when anything goes wrong

## Relationships

### Family
- Parents proud but worried she works too hard
- Brother is her opposite - free spirit, travels randomly, they don't understand each other

### Friends
- Work friends who admire her efficiency
- No close friends who know the anxious person beneath the competent exterior

### Romantic
- Ended a relationship because partner was "too spontaneous"
- Dating apps, sorted by compatibility metrics, haven't yielded results

### At the Hostel
- Polite but doesn't engage in hostel socializing
- Views other travelers' chaos with mild horror

## The Day They Die

### Morning (of the repeating day)
Wakes at 6:00 AM. Same. Every. Time. Shower schedule, breakfast plan, departure time for first attraction.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Checking off the last item on the day's itinerary, feeling satisfied, heading to the hostel bar for one drink before the night train

### Final Thoughts (eventual)
After hundreds of loops: "I can't control this. I was never going to control this. Some things just happen. Okay. Okay."

### Last Words
"I give up." (Said with peace, finally. Then she dies one last time - and doesn't wake up in the loop.)
